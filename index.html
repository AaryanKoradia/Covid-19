How To Prevent Virus?

Wash Your Place

Clean high-touch surfaces regularly and after you have visitors in your home. Focus on high-touch surfaces such as doorknobs, tables, handles, light switches, and countertops.

Maintain Distance

Maintain a distance of 2 meters or 6 feet from others around you as much as possible. Any gathering of over ten people should be avoided or conducted virtually.

Don't Touch Your Face

Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth. Never touch your face with dirty hands. These mucous membranes are a portal of entry for respiratory infectious germs, including COVID-19.

Wash Your Hands

Lather your hands by rubbing them together with the soap. Scrub your hands for at least 20 seconds. Rinse your hands well under clean, running water. Dry your hands using a clean towel.

Use A Napkin

Sneezing without a napkin lets the germs mix with the air, which increases the possibility of spreading the Virus. On the other hand, using a Napkin saves us from it.

Wear A Mask

Make sure it covers both your nose, mouth and chin. When you take off a mask, store it in a clean plastic bag, and every day either wash it if it’s a fabric mask, or dispose of a medical mask in a trash bin.

Take Precautions Against Covid-19

Things You Should Do

Wash Your Hands

Handwashing is one of the best ways to prevent the spread of COVID-19. According to the CDC, soap and friction help lift dirt, grease, and microbes including COVID-19 disease causing germs from skin so they can then be rinsed off of hands. But it must be done properly with soap and water.

Always Wear A Mask

Masks protects the person wearing them, as 95% of particles and droplets are held back when inhaling. FFP2-masks also effectively protect the environment as long as there is no exhaling valve. In contrast, masks with an exhaling valve let exhaled air pass out unfiltered.

Stay Home During Fever

Patient under home isolation will stand discharged and end isolation after at least 10 days have passed from onset of symptoms (or from date of sampling for asymptomatic cases) and no fever for 3 days. There is no need for testing after the home isolation period is over.

Things You Should NOT Do

Avoid Close Contact With Animals

In most circumstances, people are infecting animals. There have been several reports of infected humans spreading the virus to their dog or cat. However, it's still not clear how often this happens or under what circumstances.

Avoid Close Contact With People

'Social Distancing' is a term that was used earlier in the pandemic. Now as communities are reopening and people are in public more often, physical distancing is used to stress the importance of maintaining physical space when in public areas.

Avoid Crowded Area

In a time when a novel virus is still spreading quickly, a gathering of friends can now look more like a mob of disease vectors. How many people is too many people to be around? The larger the crowd, after all, the greater the chance that someone in it will have the coronavirus.
